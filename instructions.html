Before you go

  • Confirm the actual location of the trailer with the mobilization contact person.
  • Know the combination lock code or key type for the trailer (coordinate with the mobilization contact; the code is on the asset's page in this app and on its printed prep sheet).
  • Bring tools: tire pressure gauge, lug nut wrench, camera/phone.
  • For maintenance visits also bring materials: filters, engine oil, compressed air, waste-oil container, absorbent pads.
  • Open the asset in the viewer and print the Site Visit Prep Sheet — it lists the clocks you can close, open issues (bring list), and the expected inventory. Visiting several assets? Use Plan trip in the asset list to print one combined packet.
  • Kit consumables: pink zip ties, spray paint (BLUE = 50′ boom, RED = 100′ boom), red indicator tape, blank tote labels, sea foam, fuel stabilizer.

Field procedure (WRI Trailer Inspection Procedure, 2023)

Inspections are a two-person job. All results go into this app's forms on-site (details check → equipment maintenance → inventory walk, via the asset's Audit button).

  • Unload: empty front and rear compartments of loose equipment. Fast tank and skimmer may stay aboard, but inspect them for damage and confirm all parts are present and compatible.
  • Run the power equipment: start the generator and power pack to confirm function. Generator — run dry or add fuel stabilizer. Power pack — treat with sea foam and run a couple of minutes to diffuse it; do not run the power pack dry. Use the power pack to confirm the skimmer works (keep absorbents underneath to catch drips).
  • Boom count is sacred: count boom accurately, without question — un-stack, paint and count any boom not sealed and marked from a previous year. When re-stacking: buckles labeled with length, stacked tight with buckles facing out (50′ = 2 buckles, 1 spot of BLUE; 100′ = 1 buckle, RED), paint both sides and both ends.
  • Seal the boom: once re-stacked, run red indicator tape around the ratchet handle holding the deflectors so the boom can't be accessed without breaking the tape. Label the tape with boom sets, total footage, inspection date and lead inspector's initials.
  • Totes: any tote without four corner zip ties must be re-inventoried (two ties is not sufficient). One person reads the list, the other finds items — checking condition as well as quantity. Re-seal completed totes with pink zip ties on all corners; initial and date the labels; replace illegible labels.
  • Tires: record the tire manufacture date (details check in this app). Replacement cycle is 6 years from manufacture.
  • Fire extinguishers: exchange, or test and recharge. Record count and size in the details check.
  • Buoys: inflate all buoys before leaving.
  • DOT: a certified inspector performs the DOT on-site (or approved mechanic); simultaneously a WRI employee visually inspects for damage and documents it. Upload the DOT form with the exact date performed. Track the paperwork: 1 copy stays with the trailer, 1 uploaded, 1 retained for records.
  • Repairs: anything we can fix gets fixed on the current mission; otherwise schedule the repair or replacement before leaving the site.
  • Re-pack with care — everything secured and fit for travel on rough roads. The skimmer drum/wheel must not be strapped against the trailer wall; block it off with a chunk of wood.

Before you leave the site

  • Photos (required by the audit form): 1) area with the trailer in frame, 2) front with the trailer ID visible, 3) back with the license plate visible. Extras worth adding: VIN, GPS unit, asset tag, any damage.
  • Trailer swept clean and as dry as possible; rear door non-slip coating touched up if time allows.
  • Wheels chocked, jack stand padded if necessary, trailer stable for long-term storage.
  • All doors zip-tied with pink zip ties.
  • Update the asset's location/details in this app if the trailer moved; submit every form before leaving signal range (or use the Survey123 field app offline).
  • Return to the office with: completed forms submitted, a copy of the DOT certificate, updated location, and trip receipts.
